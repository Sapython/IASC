Study of Some of the Medicinal Plants Used by the Kols for the treatment of diabetes.

Introduction

Shankargarh is hilly rocky area, located 55 km away from south of Allahabad district. The study aimed to explore the concept of Health and illness among the Kol tribe at Shankargarh, in Allahabad district. Besides, it also identified the facilitators of being and staying healthy. The sample consisted of 17 males and eight females. The data were collected through semi-structured interview schedule tapped information regarding demographic and open-ended questions about meaning of Health and illness, causes of illness, faith healing and consequences of illness. Results were obtained using the thematic analysis of qualitative data. Six Global themes were identified, namely the meaning of Health, illness representation, Belief about treatment, Faith healing, Perceived cause of illness, Perceived susceptibility. The finding of such research may help in preparing intervention strategies based on a deep understanding of culture-specific factors. The role of faith healing is seen on the Health of the Kol tribe, which indicates that incorporation of faith based intervention may enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of the medical-based intervention.

Medicinal Plants used for Treatment of Diabetes by the Kols

Diabetes mellitus is caused due to deficiency in production of insulin by the pancreas, or by the ineffectiveness of the insulin produced. It is a global problem and number of those affected is increasing day by day. The plants provide a potential source of hypoglycemic drugs because many plants and plant derived compounds have been used in the treatment of diabetes. Several medicinal plants have found potential use as hypoglycemic in the Indian system of medicines, including ayurveda. Many Indian plants have been investigated for their beneficial use in different types of diabetes and reports occur in numerous scientific journals. This article aims to provide a comprehensive review on various plant species from Indian biosphere and their constituents, which have been shown to display potent hypoglycemic activity. Ayurveda and other Indian literature mention the use of plants in treatment of various human ailments. India has about 45000 plant species and among them, several thousands have been claimed to possess medicinal properties. Research conducted in last few decades on plants mentioned in ancient literature or used traditionally for diabetes have shown anti-diabetic property. Indian plants which are most effective and the most commonly studied in relation to diabetes and their complications are: Allium cepa, Allium sativum, Aloe vera , Cajanus Cajan , Coccinia indica , Caesalpinia bonducella ,Ficus bengalenesis , Gymnema sylvestre, Momordica charantia , Ocimum sanctum, Pterocarpus marsupium, Swertia chirayita ,Syzigium cumini, Tinospora cordifolia and Trigonella foenum graecum. Among these we have evaluated Stevia, Fenugreek(Trigonella foenum-graecum) ,Madagascar Periwinkle(Catharanthus roseus).All plants have shown varying degree of hypoglycemic and anti-hyperglycemic activity.

Stevia

Stevia, also called Stevia rebaudiana, is a plant that is a member of the chrysanthemum family, a subgroup of the Asteraceae family (ragweed family). Stevia is a low-calorie sweetner that has antioxidant and antibiotic properties.Its been approved by the U.S. food and drug Administration(FDA).Unlike Artificial sweetners and sugar,stevia can suppress your plasma glucose levels and significantly increase glucose tolerance.

Fenugreek

Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum) is a plant that stands around 2–3 feet (60–90 cm) tall. reducing the risk of certain cancers. The amino-acid 4 hydroxy isoleucine present in the fenugreek seeds has facilitator action on insulin secretion. In addition, fiber in the seeds helps lower rate of glucose absorption in the intestines thus controls blood sugar levels. The seeds are therefore recommended in the diabetic diet Cinnamon is also used for curing the following diseases like Diabetes. Heart Disease Urinary Infections etc.

Madagascar Periwinkle

Sadabahar also known as the ‘ever-blooming blossom’ is a well-known plant that has secured its place both in modern medicine and herbal remedies. Coming from the Apocynacea family, Sadabahar goes by the botanical name Catharanthus roseus. The plant shows extensive health benefits and its leaves are used for treating a host of health anomalies including diabetes. The excellent hypoglycaemic property of Sadabahar plays a significant role in alleviating blood sugar. The production of insulin from the β-pancreatic cells becomes active in taking this flower extract. It helps to reduce the breakdown of starch into glucose which in turn leads to low blood glucose levels.
